Information Security

In general, only officers, employees or contractors of Flive may view Personal Information.

 

Personal Information may be stored in either hard copy form or on computer or both. Hard copy Personal Information is wherever practical, kept under locked security. Personal Information stored on computer is wherever practical, password protected.

Flive uses commercially acceptable processes and procedures to protect your Personal Information from unauthorised disclosure. These processes and procedures are regularly reviewed to ensure that they are current and relevant.  However, Flive cannot guarantee that its systems will be free from unauthorised third party activity.

This means that your information will be encrypted in line with industry standards. If you have any questions regarding our security policy, please contact help@fliveapp.com

Your access to your Personal Information

You may access your own Personal Information. Flive may impose a reasonable charge for access.

 

Access to your Personal Information may be withheld in a number of circumstances. These include where:

(a) providing access would pose a serious and imminent threat to the life or health of a person;

(b) providing access would have an unreasonable impact on the privacy of others;

(c) the information is subject to confidentiality where the person who provided the information to Flive did so expressly on the condition that it remains confidential;

(d) the request is vexatious or frivolous;

(e) the information relates to current or anticipated legal proceedings between Flvie and the person and the information would not be required to be discovered to a court;

(f) providing access would be unlawful or Flive is required by a law to withhold access; or

(g) providing access could prejudice the investigation or detection by Flive or by a government body of an unlawful activity or some serious or improper misconduct.

 

Where Flive does withhold Personal Information, Flive may instead choose to give you a summary of that information. It will also provide written reasons as to why the information has been withheld.
